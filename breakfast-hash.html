Breakfast Hash - Turkey & Sweet Potato

Based on Week 1's popular Turkey Sweet Potato Hash, but with less onion and more vegetables as requested!

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs ground turkey (93% lean)
  • 2 large Japanese sweet potatoes (or regular), cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cups spinach or kale
  • 1/2 small onion, finely diced (minimal)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 4-6 eggs (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Prep vegetables: Cube sweet potatoes into 1/2-inch pieces (keeps them from getting mushy). Dice bell pepper and onion finely.
  2. Par-cook sweet potatoes: Place cubed sweet potatoes in microwave-safe bowl with 2 tbsp water. Cover and microwave 4-5 minutes until slightly tender. Drain.
  3. Brown turkey: Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in large skillet over medium-high. Add ground turkey, breaking it up. Cook 5-7 minutes until no pink remains. Season with paprika, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Remove and set aside.
  4. Saut√© vegetables: In same pan, add remaining 1 tbsp oil. Add sweet potatoes, bell pepper, and onion. Cook 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until potatoes are tender and slightly crispy.
  5. Add garlic: Add minced garlic, cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  6. Combine: Return turkey to pan. Add spinach/kale. Stir until greens wilt, 2-3 minutes.
  7. Taste and adjust: Add more salt/pepper if needed.
  8. Serve: Top with fried or poached eggs if desired.

Portion Sizes

Alexander: 2 cups hash (10-12 oz)

Hanna: 1.5 cups hash (8 oz)

Add 2 eggs per person for extra protein if desired.

üîÑ Variations

  • Protein swap: Use ground beef, pork, or chicken sausage
  • Veggie swap: Substitute butternut squash or regular potatoes for sweet potatoes
  • Add-ins: Mushrooms, zucchini, or cherry tomatoes in summer
  • Spice it up: Add red pepper flakes or hot sauce
  • Fresh finish: Top with avocado slices or fresh herbs

Meal Prep

Stores 4-5 days refrigerated. Reheat in skillet 5 minutes or microwave 2-3 minutes.

Pro tip: Pre-cubed sweet potatoes save time, but fresh-cut taste better!
