Thai Coconut Curry

Gluten-FreeDairy-FreeEgg-Free
Week 2 • Tue/Wed Dinner
Cuisine: Thai Style: One Pot Prep: 10 min Cook: 30 min Total: 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs pork shoulder OR chicken thighs, cubed
  • 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (gluten-free)
  • 1 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 cups dry jasmine rice (for serving)
  • Vegetables:
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/2 small yellow onion, sliced (minimal)

Instructions (Quantities Inline)

  1. Prep: Cut 1.5 lbs protein (pork shoulder or chicken thighs) into 1-inch cubes. Slice 1 red bell pepper, 1/2 small yellow onion, and prep 1 cup snap peas.
  2. Cook rice: Rinse 2 cups jasmine rice. Add to a pot with 3 cups water. Bring to a boil, reduce to simmer, cover, and cook for 18 minutes. Fluff and set aside.
  3. Sear protein: Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high. Brown the meat in batches, 6-8 minutes total. Remove and set aside.
  4. Make curry base: In the same pot, whisk together 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk, 2 tbsp red curry paste, 1 tbsp fish sauce, 1 tbsp coconut aminos, and 1 tsp brown sugar until smooth.
  5. Simmer: Add 1 cup chicken broth and the seared protein. Bring to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 15 minutes (pork) or 10 minutes (chicken) until tender.
  6. Add vegetables: Add the sliced red bell pepper, snap peas, and yellow onion. Simmer uncovered for 5 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp.
  7. Double batch for Wednesday: Portion half into containers for tomorrow's dinner.

Portion Sizes (per meal)

Alexander: 8 oz protein + 1.5 cups rice + generous curry sauce + vegetables

Hanna: 6 oz protein + 1 cup rice + curry sauce + vegetables

⚡ Important: Double Batch!

This recipe makes dinner for Tuesday AND Wednesday.

Portion Wednesday's meals into containers immediately after cooking. Refrigerate overnight. Reheat Wednesday 3-4 minutes in microwave or 10 minutes in saucepan.

Pork shoulder becomes tender and shreddable; chicken thighs stay juicy. Both work great!
