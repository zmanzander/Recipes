Oven Roasted Spatchcock Chicken with Herbs

Gluten-FreeDairy-FreeEgg-FreeSoy-FreeLow-FODMAP
Protein: ChickenStyle: Oven RoastedTime: Prep: 20 min | Cook: 60 min

🛡️ Medical Protocol Adaptations (Low-FODMAP)

  • Shallots: Replace with the green tops of chives or green onions to maintain flavor while staying Low-FODMAP.
  • Stone Fruit: Removed peaches per protocol; boosted lemon and fresh herbs for flavor depth.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (3-4 pounds, spatchcocked)
  • 1/4 cup garlic-infused olive oil
  • 2 whole Lemons (1 sliced into rounds, 1 for juicing)
  • 1 teaspoon Celtic sea salt
  • 1/2 cup green onion tops (sliced)
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 6 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prep: Position chicken breast side down and cut out backbone. Flip over and press on chest bone to flatten (spatchcock).
  2. Season: Rub with 2 tbsp of the infused oil, season generously with salt. Squeeze the juice of one lemon over the skin.
  3. Arrange: Place chicken in a heavy roasting pan or cast iron skillet. Tuck lemon slices, green onion tops, thyme, and rosemary sprigs around and slightly under the bird. Drizzle remaining 2 tbsp oil over the herbs and lemons.
  4. Roast: Preheat oven to 400°F. Roast for 55-60 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 165°F and the skin is golden and crispy.
  5. Serve: Serve with the roasted lemon slices and pan drippings. Garnish with fresh parsley.

Portioning Guide

Alexander: 1/2 chicken + roasted lemons.

Hanna: 1/4 chicken + roasted lemons.

Notes

Spatchcocking is the secret to even cooking and maximum crispy skin. The roasted lemon slices become soft and slightly caramelized, perfect for squeezing over the meat at the table.
