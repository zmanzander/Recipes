Pan-Roasted Pork Tenderloin with Fresh Chimichurri

Gluten-FreeDairy-FreeEgg-FreeSoy-Free
Week 3 • Wednesday Guest Dinner (3 People)
Cuisine: Latin-Inspired Style: Dinner Party Fresh Cook Prep: 10 min Cook: 15 min Total: 25 min
Guest Dinner: This elegant dish looks impressive but comes together in 25 minutes. The bright green chimichurri adds a vibrant, chef-quality presentation perfect for entertaining.

Ingredients

Pork

  • 2 Pork Tenderloins (1.5-2 lbs total for 22 oz cooked)
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper

Fresh Chimichurri Sauce

  • 1 cup Fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp Red wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup Olive oil
  • 1 tsp Red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 tsp Salt

Sides

  • 1.5 lbs Baby potatoes
  • 1 bunch Asparagus

Instructions

Step 1: Make Chimichurri (10 min)

  1. Finely chop 1 cup parsley and 1/2 cup cilantro.
  2. In a bowl, combine herbs with 4 minced garlic cloves, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, 1/2 cup olive oil, 1 tsp red pepper flakes, and 1/2 tsp salt.
  3. Mix well and set aside (flavors develop while pork cooks).

Step 2: Roast Vegetables (20 min)

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Halve 1.5 lbs baby potatoes. Toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on sheet pan.
  3. Roast for 10 minutes, then add 1 bunch asparagus (trimmed) to the pan. Roast another 10 minutes.

Step 3: Sear & Roast Pork (15 min)

  1. Pat 2 pork tenderloins dry. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Sear pork on all sides until golden brown (about 6-8 minutes total).
  4. Transfer skillet to oven. Roast at 425°F for 10-12 minutes until internal temp reaches 145°F.
  5. Remove and let rest 5 minutes before slicing into medallions.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Slice pork into 1/2-inch medallions and arrange on serving platter.
  2. Drizzle generously with chimichurri sauce.
  3. Serve with roasted potatoes and asparagus on the side.

Portion Summary (Cooked)

Alexander: 10 oz pork + sides

Hanna: 6 oz pork + sides

Guest: 6 oz pork + sides

Pro Tips

Make ahead: Chimichurri can be made 2-3 hours ahead and refrigerated (brings out the flavors even more).

Leftovers: Extra chimichurri keeps for 5 days and is amazing on eggs, grilled vegetables, or steak.
