Crispy Skin Chicken Thighs with Lemon-Caper Sauce

Gluten-FreeEgg-Free
Week 3 • Thursday Fresh Cook
Cuisine: Italian-Inspired (Piccata Style) Style: Quick Weeknight Fresh Cook Prep: 5 min Cook: 20 min Total: 25 min
Why This Works: After Monday's sweet Honey Garlic Chicken Breast, these thighs offer a savory, crispy, acidic contrast. Bone-in thighs stay juicy and the lemon-caper sauce keeps it bright and spring-like.

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 4-6 bone-in, skin-on Chicken Thighs (1-1.2 lbs meat weight)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1 tbsp Olive oil

Lemon-Caper Sauce

  • 1 Lemon (juice and zest)
  • 3 tbsp Capers, drained
  • 3/4 cup Chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp Cold butter
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced

Sides

  • 1 cup Snap peas (sautéed) OR
  • 3 cups Arugula salad

Instructions

Step 1: Crisp the Chicken (15 min)

  1. Pat 4-6 chicken thighs very dry with paper towels (key for crispy skin!).
  2. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Place thighs skin-side down. Don't move them! Cook for 8-10 minutes until skin is deeply golden and crispy.
  5. Flip and cook another 6-8 minutes until internal temp reaches 165°F.
  6. Remove thighs to a plate and tent with foil.

Step 2: Make the Sauce (5 min)

  1. In the same skillet (don't clean!), reduce heat to medium.
  2. Add 2 minced garlic cloves. Cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add 3/4 cup chicken stock, juice of 1 lemon, lemon zest, and 3 tbsp capers.
  4. Simmer for 3-4 minutes until reduced by half and slightly thickened.
  5. Remove from heat. Stir in 2 tbsp cold butter until melted and glossy.
  6. Add 2 tbsp fresh parsley.

Step 3: Serve

  1. Return chicken to skillet and spoon sauce over the top.
  2. Serve with sautéed snap peas or a light arugula salad on the side.

Portion Summary (Cooked)

Alexander: 10 oz chicken (3 large thighs) + sides

Hanna: 6 oz chicken (2 medium thighs) + sides

Pro Tips

Crispy skin secret: Dry chicken + don't move it while cooking = perfect crisp.

Sauce variation: Add a splash of white wine before the stock for extra depth (optional).

Make it lighter: Serve over arugula instead of heavier sides to keep it spring-fresh.
