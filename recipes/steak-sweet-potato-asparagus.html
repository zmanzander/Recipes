Grilled Steak with Roasted Yukon Gold Potatoes & Asparagus

Gluten-FreeDairy-FreeEgg-FreeSoy-FreeLow-FODMAP
Week 3 • Sunday Fresh Cook
Cuisine: American Style: BBQ Fresh Cook Prep: 10 min Cook: 20 min Total: 30 min Serves: 2

What You're Making

Sunday Dinner: Simple grilled steaks with roasted Yukon Gold potatoes and asparagus.

Ingredients

Proteins

  • 1.5 lbs Steak (Ribeye or Sirloin)

Sides

  • 2 large Yukon Gold Potatoes
  • 1 bunch Asparagus

Seasoning

  • Olive oil
  • Sea salt & Black pepper
  • Rosemary (fresh or dried)
  • Optional:

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Grill

  1. Preheat your barbecue to medium-high heat (around 450-500°F).
  2. Clean and oil the grates to prevent sticking.

Step 2: Roast Yukon Gold Potatoes & Asparagus (20 min)

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Cube 2 large Yukon Gold potatoes and toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a sheet pan.
  3. Roast for 15 minutes.
  4. Trim 1 bunch asparagus. Add to the sheet pan (or a separate pan) and toss with olive oil/salt. Roast for an additional 10-12 minutes until everything is tender.

Step 3: Grill the Steaks (10-12 min)

  1. Pat 1.5 lbs steak dry with paper towels.
  2. Brush both sides lightly with olive oil.
  3. Season generously with sea salt, black pepper, and rosemary (and if using).
  4. Place steaks on the hot grill. Close the lid.
  5. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare (internal temp 130-135°F), or 6-7 minutes per side for medium (internal temp 140-145°F).
  6. Remove from grill and let rest for 5 minutes before slicing against the grain.

Step 4: Plate & Enjoy

  1. Alexander's plate: 10 oz steak + 1.5 cups Yukon Gold potato + heavy asparagus.
  2. Hanna's plate: 6 oz steak + 1 cup Yukon Gold potato + moderate asparagus.

Grilling Tips

Resting: Resting the steak is crucial for juicy meat. The internal temp will rise 5°F during the rest.

Flare-ups: If you get flare-ups from fat dripping, move the steak to a cooler part of the grill temporarily.

Grill marks: For perfect crosshatch marks, rotate the steak 45° halfway through each side.

Don't flip too early: Let the steak develop a crust before flipping (it will release naturally from the grates when ready).
