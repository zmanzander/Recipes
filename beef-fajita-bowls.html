Beef Fajita Bowls

Week 2 • Thursday Dinner
Cuisine: Mexican Style: Sheet Pan + Stovetop Prep: 15 min Cook: 20 min Total: 35 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs (24 oz) flank or skirt steak, sliced thin against the grain
  • 3 bell peppers (mix of red, yellow, orange), sliced into strips
  • 1 large onion, sliced into strips (moderate amount)
  • 2 cups dry jasmine rice (yields 6 cups cooked)
  • 2 ripe avocados, sliced
  • 1/2 bunch cilantro, chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • 1 cup salsa
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fajita seasoning:
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Instructions (Quantities Inline)

  1. Cook rice: Rinse 2 cups dry jasmine rice. Add to a pot with 3 cups water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cover for 18 minutes. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Prep vegetables: Slice 3 bell peppers and 1 large onion into similar-sized strips.
  3. Season steak: In a small bowl, mix 1 tbsp cumin, 1 tbsp chili powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Toss the 1.5 lbs (24 oz) thinly sliced steak with the seasoning until coated.
  4. Sear steak: Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet over high heat. Cook steak in batches for 2-3 minutes per side until charred. Do not overcrowd. Remove and set aside.
  5. Sauté vegetables: In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tbsp oil. Cook the peppers and onion for 8-10 minutes until softened and slightly charred.
  6. Final Prep: While vegetables cook, slice 2 avocados and chop 1/2 bunch cilantro. Cut 2 limes into wedges.
  7. Assemble bowls: Start with rice, then top with steak, fajita vegetables, 1/4 cup salsa, 1/2 sliced avocado, cilantro, and a lime squeeze per person.

Portion Sizes (Cooked Targets)

Alexander: 8 oz cooked steak + 1.5 cups rice + 2 cups vegetables + 1/2 avocado + 1/4 cup salsa

Hanna: 6 oz cooked steak + 1 cup rice + 1.5 cups vegetables + 1/2 avocado + 1/4 cup salsa

Notes

Slice against the grain! This is critical for tender steak. Look for the lines in the meat and cut perpendicular to them.

High heat = better char. Don't crowd the pan or the steak will steam instead of sear.

For extra heat, add sliced jalapeños with the peppers.
