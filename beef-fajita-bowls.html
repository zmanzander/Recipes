Low-FODMAP Beef Fajita Bowls

Week 3 • Mon-Thu Lunch Prep
Style: Savory Meal Prep (Low-FODMAP) Prep: 15 min Cook: 25 min Total: 40 min Makes: 8 portions (Mon-Thu x 2 people)

Ingredients

  • 2.5 lbs (40 oz) flank or sirloin steak, sliced thin against the grain
  • 4 bell peppers (mix of red, yellow, orange), sliced into strips
  • 2 bunches Spring Onions (GREEN PARTS ONLY - discard white bulbs)
  • 3 cups dry jasmine rice (for the week's lunches)
  • Garlic-infused olive oil
  • Seasoning: 2 tbsp Cumin, 1 tbsp Chili powder (check for garlic/onion ingredients), 1 tsp Smoked paprika, 1.5 tsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper.
  • Serving: Fresh cilantro, Lime wedges, Avocado (limit to 1/8th per meal).

Instructions (Quantities Inline)

  1. Cook rice: Rinse 3 cups dry jasmine rice 2-3 times. Add to a pot with 4.5 cups water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cover for 18-20 minutes. Fluff and let cool before portioning.
  2. Prep vegetables: Slice 4 bell peppers into strips. Slice the green tops only of 2 bunches of spring onions.
  3. Season steak: In a small bowl, mix 2 tbsp cumin, 1 tbsp chili powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1.5 tsp salt, and 1 tsp black pepper. Toss the 2.5 lbs thinly sliced steak with the seasoning and 1 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil.
  4. Sear steak: Heat 1 tbsp garlic-infused oil in a large skillet over high heat. Cook steak in batches for 2-3 minutes per side until charred. Do not overcrowd. Remove and set aside.
  5. Sauté vegetables: In the same pan, add another splash of garlic-infused oil if needed. Cook the peppers and spring onion greens for 5-8 minutes until softened and slightly charred.
  6. Portion & Store:
    • Alexander (4 containers): 8-10 oz cooked steak + 1.5 cups rice + 1.5 cups vegetables.
    • Hanna (4 containers): 5-6 oz cooked steak + 1 cup rice + 1 cup vegetables.

Target Weights (Cooked)

Alexander: 8-10 oz steak + 1.5 cups rice + heavy vegetables.

Hanna: 5-6 oz steak + 1 cup rice + moderate vegetables.

Low-FODMAP Reheating

Oven Only: 350°F for 10 minutes. Top with fresh cilantro and a heavy lime squeeze after reheating to brighten the flavors.

Important: Ensure your chili powder is a pure blend (no added onion or garlic powder) to remain strictly Low-FODMAP.
