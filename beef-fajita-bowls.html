Beef Fajita Bowls

Week 2 ‚Ä¢ Thursday Dinner
Cuisine: Mexican Style: Sheet Pan + Stovetop Prep: 15 min Cook: 20 min Total: 35 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs flank or skirt steak, sliced thin against the grain
  • 3 bell peppers (mix of red, yellow, orange), sliced into strips
  • 1 large onion, sliced into strips (moderate amount)
  • 2 cups dry jasmine rice (yields 6 cups cooked)
  • 2 ripe avocados, sliced
  • 1/2 bunch cilantro, chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • 1 cup salsa (your favorite brand)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fajita seasoning:
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Cook rice: 2 cups dry rice + 3 cups water. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer, cover 18 minutes. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Prep vegetables: Slice peppers and onion into similar-sized strips.
  3. Season steak: Mix cumin, chili powder, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Coat steak slices generously.
  4. Sear steak: Heat 1 tbsp oil in large skillet over high heat. Cook steak in batches 2-3 minutes per side for char. Don't overcrowd. Remove and set aside.
  5. Saut√© vegetables: In same pan, add remaining 1 tbsp oil. Cook peppers and onion 8-10 minutes until softened with some char.
  6. Slice avocado and chop cilantro while vegetables cook.
  7. Assemble bowls: Rice base + steak + fajita vegetables + salsa + avocado slices + cilantro + lime squeeze.

Portion Sizes

Alexander: 8 oz steak + 1.5 cups rice + 2 cups vegetables + 1/2 avocado + 1/4 cup salsa

Hanna: 6 oz steak + 1 cup rice + 1.5 cups vegetables + 1/2 avocado + 1/4 cup salsa

Notes

Slice against the grain! This is critical for tender steak. Look for the lines in the meat and cut perpendicular to them.

High heat = better char. Don't crowd the pan or the steak will steam instead of sear.

For extra heat, add sliced jalape√±os with the peppers.
