Beef Stir-Fry with Broccoli

Cuisine: Asian Style: Quick Fresh Cook Prep: 15 min Cook: 15 min Best: Eaten fresh

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs sirloin steak, sliced thin against the grain
  • 2 tbsp GF soy sauce or coconut aminos (for marinade)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (for marinade)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper (for marinade)
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup GF soy sauce or coconut aminos (for sauce)
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (for sauce)
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil (avocado or coconut)
  • Cooked white rice for serving

Instructions

  1. Toss sliced sirloin with 2 tbsp GF soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, and black pepper. Set aside while prepping vegetables.
  2. Cut broccoli, bell peppers, and onion. Mince garlic and ginger.
  3. Mix all sauce ingredients (1/4 cup soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, 1 tsp sesame oil, red pepper flakes) in a small bowl.
  4. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add beef in batches. Sear 1-2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  5. Add remaining oil to pan. Add broccoli first (cook 3 min), then bell peppers and onion (cook 3-4 min). Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute.
  6. Return beef to pan. Pour sauce over everything. Toss to combine and cook 2 minutes until sauce thickens.
  7. Serve immediately over rice.

Portion Sizes

Alexander: 8 oz beef + 1.5 cups vegetables + 1.5 cups rice

Wife: 5 oz beef + 1 cup vegetables + 1 cup rice

Notes

Best eaten fresh (doesn't meal prep well). Slice beef THIN and against the grain for tenderness. Cook on HIGH heat — this is key to good stir-fry. Don't overcrowd the pan or beef will steam instead of sear.
